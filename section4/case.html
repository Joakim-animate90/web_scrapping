DECISION


1. DAVANI .J: By Notice of Motion filed on 14 May, 2006, the Respondent seeks orders that the appeal be dismissed for want of prosecution and that the Madang District Court’s Order of 27 May, 2003, take effect.


Background Facts


2. On 27 May, 2003, the Madang District Court made orders that judgment be entered against the appellant Joe Jeffrey, to pay the respondent Roy Agal trading as Rohean Electrical and Refrigeration, (‘Roy Agal’), the sum of K4,620.20 together with interest and costs.


3. On 27 June, 2003, the appellant filed an appeal appealing against this decision. Together with the appeal, he filed Entry of Appeal and Recognizance of Appeal. At that time, he had the assistance of Acanufa & Associates, Lawyers.


4. Since that time the appellant has not taken any further steps in the prosecution of the appeal.



This application


5. This application to dismiss is supported by the affidavit of Roy Agal sworn on 9 April, 2006, which affidavit states briefly that the appellant had not done anything or taken steps to prosecute the appeal since filing the appeal on 27 June, 2003. The appellant has not filed any affidavits in reply.



6. The appellant was personally served with the application on 8 May, 2006. The affidavit of service of Konmai Kindi sworn on 9 May, 2006 attests to that.


7. According to the endorsements on the court file, the appellant was given several opportunities to settle the matter or take steps to have the appeal set down for hearing.



8. The endorsements read;


(i) 10 May, 2006: Both appellant and respondent appeared before Los .J. The motion was moved and the appellant asked the court for an adjournment of 30 days to find a lawyer. The court gave him seven (7) days within which to do that and also ordered that if this did not occur, that the court would hear the motion to dismiss.


(ii) 19 May, 2006: Mr Illaisa of Stevens Lawyers appeared for Roy Agal, the Respondent/Applicant. The appellant did not appear. Mr Illaisa informed the court that there was a possibility of an out of court settlement.


(iii) 10th August, 2006: Both parties appeared before me. They did not have lawyers. The appellant asked for time to brief a lawyer. I adjourned to 22 August, 2006 for the hearing of the application.


(iv) 22 August, 2006: Both parties appeared. The appellant informed the court that he spoke to a lawyer about three weeks ago. I noted that a Notice of Appearance had not been filed, nor any other materials in the way of affidavits.


I ruled that the respondent will move his application because the appellant had sufficient time to find a lawyer or file materials in response but had not done so. Furthermore, that he was not genuine in those requests and was deliberately delaying the hearing of the motion or the execution of the judgment.


Evidence and the Law


9. The law on dismissal of proceedings for want of prosecution is well settled in this jurisdiction. The court will do so where the plaintiffs default has been intentional and contumelious and where there has been inordinate and inexcusable delay. (See Ronald Nicholas v Commonwealth New Guinea Timbers Pty. Ltd [1986] PNGLR 133).


10. It has been three (3) years since the District Court made its decision and the appeal was filed, in May and June, 2003, respectively. Since then, the appellant has not taken any steps to prosecute the appeal. He has not shown any reasons as to why this has occurred. Apart from his representations to find a lawyer, such representations having been made in May, 2006 and August 2006, the appellantsappellant’s non-prosecution of the appeal are clearly intentional, inexcusable and done with a view to prevent the execution of the judgment.





Orders



11. Having found that to be so, the court will, in the exercise of its discretion, order that the appeal be dismissed for want of prosecution. The court’s formal orders are;


1. The appeal be dismissed in its entirety;


2. The District Court’s orders of 27 May, 2003 take effect immediately;


  1. The appellant to pay the respondents reasonable costs of the appeal and this application, assessed at K500.00;


  1. Time is abridged to time of settlement to take place forthwith.


12. As to the ordered costs, I say K500.00 because the only time the respondent appeared in court is in relation to this application
